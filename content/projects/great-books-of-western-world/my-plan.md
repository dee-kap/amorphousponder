# My Plan For Reading The Great Books of Western World

The Great Books of Western World comprises of approximately 2500 years of selected knowledge compiled into 54 volumes. Reading the books is a great way to self educate one self in liberal arts. As an aspiring generalist, I foresee great value in it for myself and I'm a bit too excited to go through these volumes. 

I have figured out that I will need more than just excitment. I will need a reading plan to extract the maximum value for the effort I will put into this project. 

There are others who have published their reading plans online and they are all excellent plans to follow. I could follow one of them but that will be a passive effort. One thing I have learned over the years is that any learning effort must be active. So I have decided to create my own plan for reading The Great Books. 

My plan is to read the volumes in sequential order (take that for active) starting with of course [The Great Conversation](vol-1-the-great-conversation/index.md) which is volume 1 and then over the years get to the final 54 which is [Sigmund Freud](vol-54-freud/index.md).
